Your Phoenix Roof Replacement Timeline

Know exactly what to expect — day by day — so there are no surprises when your crew shows up.

🚛
Day 1: Material Delivery
Flatbed truck · Tiles & underlayment · Driveway staging

🚛 Day 1

Material Delivery

What to Expect: A heavy-duty flatbed truck will arrive to drop off your new underlayment, flashing, and replacement tiles or shingles. The materials will be staged on your driveway and possibly on the roof itself.

Your Action: Park all vehicles on the street before Day 1. Your driveway must remain completely clear for delivery trucks and safe material staging. You do not need to be home for delivery.

🔨
Day 2: Tear-Off & Structural Seal
Old felt removed · Deck inspected · New underlayment laid

🔨 Day 2

The Tear-Off & Structural Seal

What to Expect: This is the loudest day of construction. The crew will strip your roof down to the bare wood deck, inspect the plywood for hidden water damage, and immediately lay down high-grade waterproof underlayment to seal the house.

If any plywood decking is found to be rotted or compromised from years of moisture, the crew will replace those sections before sealing. Your contractor will walk you through anything they find.

Your Action: Keep pets indoors — the noise and foot traffic can be stressful for animals. Construction typically begins around 6:00 AM to beat the intense Arizona heat. You don't need to be home, but be available by phone.

🏠
Day 3: Final Installation & Cleanup
Tiles locked in · Flashing finished · Magnetic nail sweep

🏠 Day 3

Final Tile Installation & Ground Sweep

What to Expect: The crew locks the tiles securely into place and finishes the valley flashing and ridge caps. Once the roof is completely sealed, they perform a thorough ground sweep using heavy-duty magnetic rollers to clear your yard and driveway of any stray construction nails.

Your contractor will do a final walkthrough with you (or send photos if you're not home) and review the completed work before the crew leaves.

Your Action: Walk the yard yourself after the crew leaves. Run a barefoot check of the grass nearest the house — magnetic rollers catch most nails but a second pass never hurts, especially if you have children or pets.

☀️ Thinking About Solar? Do It in the Right Order.

If you're considering solar panels, the best time to install them is after your new roof is complete — not before. Installing solar on an aging roof means you'll need to remove and reinstall the panels when the roof eventually fails, adding $2,000–$5,000 in extra cost. A fresh roof gives you a clean, warrantied surface and 20–25 years of worry-free solar performance. Mention it to your contractor — many have referral relationships with local solar installers.
