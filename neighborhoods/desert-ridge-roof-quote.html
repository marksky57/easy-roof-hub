Why Desert Ridge Homeowners Are Getting Quotes Now

Desert Ridge is a master-planned community in north Phoenix built primarily between 1995 and 2005. Roofs from that era are now entering or past the typical service life for asphalt shingles in the Arizona climate.

Arizona's conditions are uniquely hard on roofing materials:

  • Intense UV exposure degrades shingles faster than most of the country
  • Monsoon moisture and wind create stress and potential for hidden leaks
  • Wide daily temperature swings cause expansion and contraction over time

A free professional inspection takes the guesswork out of it — and gives you a clear picture of where your roof stands before a problem becomes expensive.

Common Roofing Materials in This Area

  • Architectural and 3-tab asphalt shingles (most common, built pre-2005)
  • Concrete and clay tile — durable, but underlayment may need replacement at 20+ years
  • Foam roofing on flat or low-slope sections
  • Some homes have a mix of materials across different roof sections

What Typically Affects Roofing Costs

  • Roof pitch and how accessible it is
  • Number of penetrations — vents, skylights, chimneys
  • Full replacement vs. targeted repair
  • Material choice: shingle, tile, or foam re-coat
  • Condition of the decking underneath
